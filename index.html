8500 units of new housing approved in Gowanus, Brooklyn. Largest rezoning under De Blasio Administration

By: Pete Tomao, Feb. 7, 2021

82 city blocks were rezoned in Gowanus to accomodate 8,500 new units of housing, 3,000 of them below market rate. City planning documents show rent in the Gowanus area has increased 36% over the past decade, almost triple the city-wide average.

The average one bedroom apartment in Gowanus rents for $2,500 per month up from $1,800 in 2012. 28% of Brooklynites spend more than 50% of their income on rent, the rezoning is helping add new affordable housing to the borough.

“We’ve obviously lost a lot of affordable housing in the community over the last several decades...it's at crisis level the need,” Michelle de la Uz, Executive Director, Fifth Avenue Committee.

The Fifth Avenue Committee is a major provider of affordable housing in Brooklyn. De la Uz notes a recent housing lottery for 86 affordable units received 67,000 applications.
